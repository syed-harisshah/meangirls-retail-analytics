# Executive Summary — The Mean Girls Store
**Period analyzed:** Feb 24 – Jul 2, 2026 · **Currency:** PKR · **Data:** 632 orders, 531 customers, 29.9K sessions

## Business snapshot

The store scaled from a standing start to PKR 1.28M/month in four months. Growth came almost entirely from order volume (61 → 299 orders/month) on a flat ~PKR 4,200 AOV, powered by social traffic (86% of sessions, 91% mobile). Cancellation rate is a healthy 3.8%.

## Five recommendations, in priority order

**1. Restock the wine-red winners immediately.**
Two products (Y2K Wine Red Henley, Laced Wine Red Trim Top) = 31% of all-time gross sales and both are at 0–5 units. At June run-rates each stocked-out day on these forfeits roughly PKR 8–10K. Reorder depth should follow the Pareto: ~8 SKUs deserve 80% of inventory capital.

**2. Launch a day-10 re-engagement flow.**
Repeat buyers make their second purchase after a median of 8 days; by day 33, 75% of those who will return already have. A WhatsApp/SMS touch at day 10–14 (new drops, styling content, small code) targets the window that matters. The 257-customer June cohort is the immediate audience — matching May's 8.6% month-1 retention is worth ~PKR 90K/month.

**3. Win back the Hibernating 45%.**
237 customers bought once during the growth push and lapsed (avg 58 days since purchase, PKR 889K historical revenue). One win-back campaign to an existing list costs near-zero versus acquiring the same revenue cold on social.

**4. Rebalance spend toward high-intent channels.**
Search converts at 4.7% vs social's 0.5%. Social should remain the reach engine, but the harvest layer is missing: basic SEO on product pages, Google Shopping, and retargeting of the 2,000+ cart-adders who didn't buy. Also: 73% of orders have no referrer attribution — add UTMs to bio/DM links before scaling paid spend.

**5. Lift AOV with bundles and thresholds.**
Growth so far is all volume. A free-shipping threshold at PKR 5,000 (current AOV 4,205) and a "complete the set" bundle (top + skirt) are the standard, low-risk plays. The tote-bag freebie already proved customers respond to add-on value (204 redemptions).

## Risks & caveats

June's surge coincided with tripled social reach; the forecast's upper path (PKR 500K/week) assumes that reach is sustained. Retention metrics for June are still censored. The May 21 bulk-order day (10 orders of PKR 11–16K) looks like a wholesale/group buy — worth investigating as a channel, but it distorts naive trend reads.
